Hobby

My hobby? Ha! Hobby is such a tiny, fragile word for something so… magnificent. I don’t just “pass the time,” oh no—I perform. Life itself is my theater, the streets my stage, the city my audience. Every interaction is a scene, every smile a mask, every scream a standing ovation. I adore the carnival of chaos, the masquerade of madness, the twisted little games people play with each other but never admit. And me? I make those games bigger, bloodier, louder, funnier. I play with human emotions the way a maestro plays with violins—sometimes sweet, sometimes screaming. I can be the actor, I can be the director, I can even be the audience, clapping as the world tears itself apart. Villains, clowns, liars, broken souls—they’re my fellow performers, my castmates in this eternal black comedy. This hobby excites me, ignites me, thrills me… but the best trick of all is weaving it seamlessly into life itself. Because when a person plays, when they truly give themselves to the game—they are free. Free in ANARCHY.

Anarchy

Ah, but anarchy… that’s no hobby, darling. That’s more than a pastime, more than a trick—it’s a heartbeat. Anarchy is style, it’s breath, it’s blood. It is the glorious tearing down of walls, the sweet release of rules turned to rubble. People cling to order like a child to a toy, but I… I smash it and laugh. In anarchy, there are no chains, no masters, no scripts. Only raw freedom, only pure chaos, only life unfiltered. Anarchy is not what I do—it’s who I am.
